Key questions we'll explore:
What are the dimensions of educational activism,
and what roles have NYC educators played within
them?
How have NYC educators, students, families, and
community members envisioned, sought, and
achieved justice since the 1930s?
How, and to what degree, have local and
national movements for educational justice
interacted?
Why do histories of educational activism for
racially minoritized groups matter, and what role
does intersectionality play in those histories?
